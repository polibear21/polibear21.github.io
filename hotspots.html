Hotspots Project

In collaboration with Lexington's Town Celebration Committee


Introduction

Lexington, MA is a town with many historic landmarks and enjoyable events such as the famous Patriots' Day parade. Unfortunately, Patriots' Day had to be virtual in 2021 due to COVID-19. The Lexington Town Governmentâ€™s Celebration committee reached out to our coding team; they wanted us to create a website with virtual events for people to have fun in despite the pandemic. Our website allowed residents to participate in multiple activities including taking photos in front of historical locations. These monuments were popular and visited very frequently during the event. Please check the Patriots' Day project page for more detail. Another major activity of the parade was that participants could mark their location on a GPS based map system for each of the monuments they visited. This helped our team collect data for future use, and inspired the Hotspots project.



What We Did

In this Hotspots project, we planned on developing a machine learning algorithm that could successfully analyze data from the 2021 Patriots' Day parade. Our team learned the Weka API and planned to code the final result in Java. The ultimate goal was to publish a research paper of our findings and improve the virtual parade for the future. However, we disbanded this project in March 2022 when the Lexington government announced their Patriots Day' parade would return in person. Also, co-leader Gabriel Sui got accepted to the University of Oxford, England and eventually graduated our team! Instead, our team joined other ongoing projects to continue helping our town!



Impact On Community

In this project, our group analyzed data collected from the 2021 virtual Patriots' Day parade to search for patterns that could improve the event in the future. Civic engagement is promoted through this event by building a sense of community, giving citizens an opportunity to go outside and visit town landmarks inspiring a sense of pride for the rich heritage of the town. Since many Lexington citizens had minimized social contact and isolated themselves during the pandemic, the Patriots' Day event also promoted taking a nice walk, run or bike ride with friends and family. The Hotspots data planned to enhance the event and encourage future engagement, further benefiting our community!
